Electronic Data Interchange (EDI)

Definition: EDI is the structured transmission of data between organizations using standardized formats. It facilitates the electronic exchange of business documents such as purchase orders, invoices, and shipping notices. Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is a technology that enables the seamless and automated exchange of business documents between organizations in a standardized electronic format. By replacing traditional paper-based communication with electronic data exchange, EDI enhances efficiency, accuracy, and speed in business transactions.


  • Increased Efficiency
  • Cost Savings
  • Enhanced Accuracy and Speed
  • Better Data Management
  • Strengthened Business Relationships
